Case Study 01 — F&B · Digital Marketing

Mirch
Masala

Turning Legacy Into Local Demand
Industry
F&B / Restaurants
Services
Digital · Performance
Timeline
3 Months
Location
Surat, India

With a 30-year legacy and strong pan-India presence, Mirch Masala launched its new Surat outlet in August. Despite the brand's credibility and established identity, the outlet struggled to generate the expected response during its initial months of operation.

The challenge wasn't the product. It was the market positioning. Located on the first floor of a commercial complex — a factor that naturally impacts walk-ins — the outlet required a completely different consumer acquisition strategy tailored for Surat's audience behavior.

The Challenge
Low organic footfall for nearly 3 months post-launch
Limited local market awareness despite strong brand recall elsewhere
Location disadvantages — first floor of commercial complex
Disconnect between brand perception and Surat's dining audience
Our Strategy

Instead of directly pushing sales-focused campaigns, we approached the market in two phases.

Phase 01
Awareness & Market Conditioning
  • Strategic content positioning and brand recall campaigns
  • Hyperlocal audience targeting specific to Surat
  • Digital visibility enhancement across platforms
  • Consumer behavior mapping for the local dining audience
Phase 02
Performance Marketing & Footfall Optimization
  • Optimized ad sets with geo-targeted promotions
  • Audience segmentation based on dining behavior
  • Retargeting strategies to convert aware audiences
  • Creative communication tailored to Surat's preferences
The Results
↑
Brand awareness in Surat increased significantly within 3 months
↑
Footfall consistency strengthened despite the first-floor location challenge
↑
Substantial increase in average sales and customer engagement

In F&B, legacy alone doesn't guarantee performance in a new market. Understanding consumer psychology, regional audience behavior, positioning, and performance marketing is what truly drives growth.

We don't just market restaurants. We engineer demand for them.

F&B / Restaurants
Services
Branding · Content
Timeline

Outcome

In the F&B industry, perception sells before the food does. When Kisna Canteen partnered with Laughing Lions, the brand was facing a critical issue — weak digital presentation. Despite having a strong product and growing customer potential, the brand's online presence lacked the visual identity and communication quality required to compete in a rapidly evolving food market.

Low-quality food photography, inconsistent content, and weak brand positioning were directly impacting engagement, audience perception, and ultimately, sales.

The Challenge
Weak digital presentation — inconsistent content and poor food photography
Brand positioning not aligned with competitive F&B market expectations
New branch launch due within one month of takeover
No scalable visual identity to support multi-location growth
Our Approach
01 — Visual
Visual Identity Upgrade
  • Complete overhaul of food shoot quality and creative direction
  • Elevated reels and short-form content production
  • Premium brand presentation across all platforms
  • Appetite-driven visual language built for recall
02 — Strategy
Strategic Content Positioning
  • Content strategy aligned with brand tone and audience behavior
  • Product highlights structured around market perception goals
  • Every post contributing toward stronger brand recall and trust
03 — Expansion
Expansion-Focused Branding
  • Visual consistency designed for multi-location scalability
  • Communication style supporting new branch launch positioning
  • Long-term brand identity built for F&B market growth
The Results
↑
Content quality transformed significantly within the first month
↑
New branch launch received stronger digital support and positioning
↑
Brand established a more premium, scalable, expansion-ready identity

Most restaurant brands focus only on posting content. We focus on building positioning. Visuals create perception, perception builds trust, and trust drives sales.

We didn't just improve their content. We prepared them for expansion.
Case Study 03 — Office Branding · Signage & Wayfinding

Credence
Wealth

Designing a Legacy Brand For Its Next Chapter
Industry
Finance / Wealth
Services
Office · Signage
Legacy
30+ Years
Scope
Full Interior Brand

After building a trusted reputation over 30+ years, Credence Wealth was entering a new phase of growth with the launch of its new office space. While the brand carried decades of credibility, the new workspace required an identity system that reflected its evolution, professionalism, and modern positioning.

This wasn't just about aesthetics. It was about translating a legacy into a physical experience.

The Challenge
New office space needed a refreshed, modern brand identity system
Visual communication had to balance legacy with modern professionalism
Interior branding required spatial storytelling, not just signage
Every corner had to communicate trust, scale, and credibility
Our Approach
01 — Identity
Brand Identity Alignment
  • Reimagined visual language suited for the next-generation workspace
  • Preserved trust and legacy while introducing modern sophistication
  • Every design decision balanced corporate professionalism with brand consistency
02 — Space
Interior Office Branding
  • Wall branding, directional graphics, and environmental design elements
  • Spatial communication systems creating an immersive brand experience
  • Premium visual installations making the office feel intentional throughout
03 — Navigation
Signage & Wayfinding Design
  • Navigation clarity and spatial hierarchy throughout the workspace
  • Visitor experience and brand visibility at every touchpoint
  • Cohesive, premium, purpose-driven signage system end-to-end
The Results
✦
Office transformed from a workspace into a fully branded experience
✦
Modern identity successfully preserved and elevated the 30-year brand legacy
✦
Stronger spatial communication and visual consistency across every touchpoint

Strong brands are not built only through digital presence — they are experienced through spaces. We specialize in creating signage and wayfinding systems that combine branding, functionality, and spatial storytelling.

We didn't design an office. We designed an environment that communicates trust.
